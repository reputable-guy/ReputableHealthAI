Study Title: A single bout of jumping exercise does not modulate serum markers of bone formation or bone resorption throughout a 24&#xa0;h period.

Study Summary:
This randomized, cross-over trial assessed the effect of a single bout of high-impact exercise on serum markers of bone formation and bone resorption over a 24&#xa0;h period. Twenty healthy males and females performed a single bout of brief jumping exercise (EXC) or no exercise (CON), 55&#xa0;min following consumption of a standard breakfast. Blood markers of bone formation (P1NP) and bone resorption (CTX-I) were assessed before (t&#xa0;=&#xa0;0&#xa0;h) and over a 5&#xa0;h period after breakfast, and following 24&#xa0;h of post-exercise recovery (t&#xa0;=&#xa0;24&#xa0;h). Serum CTX-I concentrations decreased during the 5&#xa0;h postprandial period (time-effect, P&#xa0;<&#xa0;0.001) with no differences between conditions (time x condition, P&#xa0;=&#xa0;0.14). After a&#xa0;~&#xa0;16&#xa0;% decline during the first 30&#xa0;min following breakfast, serum P1NP concentrations gradually returned to baseline values during the 5&#xa0;h postprandial period, with no differences in the overall response between conditions (time-effect, P&#xa0;<&#xa0;0.001; time x condition, P&#xa0;=&#xa0;0.25). Fasted serum CTX-I concentrations decreased from 0.33&#xa0;&#xb1;&#xa0;0.15 and 0.35&#xa0;&#xb1;&#xa0;0.15&#xa0;ng/mL at baseline, to 0.31&#xa0;&#xb1;&#xa0;0.13 and 0.31&#xa0;&#xb1;&#xa0;0.16&#xa0;ng/mL at t&#xa0;=&#xa0;24&#xa0;h in CON and EXC, respectively, with no differences between conditions (time-effect, P&#xa0;<&#xa0;0.01; time x condition, P&#xa0;=&#xa0;0.70). Fasted serum P1NP concentrations did not change from baseline to t&#xa0;=&#xa0;24&#xa0;h in both CON (baseline: 76&#xa0;&#xb1;&#xa0;27&#xa0;ng/mL, t&#xa0;=&#xa0;24&#xa0;h: 79&#xa0;&#xb1;&#xa0;26&#xa0;ng/mL) and EXC (baseline: 80&#xa0;&#xb1;&#xa0;24&#xa0;ng/mL, t&#xa0;=&#xa0;24&#xa0;h: 77&#xa0;&#xb1;&#xa0;29&#xa0;ng/mL; time-effect, P&#xa0;=&#xa0;0.89), with no differences between conditions (time x condition, P&#xa0;=&#xa0;0.22). High-impact exercise does not modulate the concentrations of the serum marker of bone formation P1NP and the serum marker of bone resorption CTX-I throughout a 24&#xa0;h recovery period in healthy adults.
